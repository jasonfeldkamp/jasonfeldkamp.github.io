Fourier Series

A script that produces an animation of several Fourier Series waveforms.

Download Code [.zip]

This script produces an animation of a Fourier Series via the addition of circles with rotating vectors. Each subsequent circle's center is attached to the tip of the rotating vector of the prior circle. By adjusting the number of circles, their radii, and the frequencies of their rotating vectors, a variety of different waveforms can be produced. This script is capable of producing n terms (circles) of the Fourier series, however, too many circles can negatively impact performance.

In the video below, a single circle with a rotating vector produces a standard sinusoid graph.


By increasing the number of circles to 3, a distinguishable waveform begins to take shape: the square wave.


The following video shows the square waveform with 20 circles.


By changing the relations between subsequent circles' radii and frequencies, different waveforms can be produced. The following video depicts the sawtooth waveform produced from 20 circles.


The last 2 videos are of more abstract waveforms that I discoved by playing around with the script. You can even begin to notice elements of common mathmatical functions within their periodic nature: notably the asymptotic nature of the tangent function and the exponential function.
